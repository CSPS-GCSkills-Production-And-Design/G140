Performance Management Annual Cycle: Throughout the Year, and at Mid-year, when Performance Is Reviewed

Introduction

Performance management has to be more than a once-a-year activity of checking off some boxes. It won’t be successful unless it’s part of a meaningful and regular dialogue with the employee throughout the year.

At the mid‑year mark, you will need to schedule, prepare and have a conversation on progress to date, and then document your employee’s performance.

Graphic - Text version

Box labelled “At mid‑year,” that contains three statements:

  • “Employee identifies any issues affecting his or her performance”; and
  • “Manager/supervisor reviews learning and development plan with employee and updates it if required.”
