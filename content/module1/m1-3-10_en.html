Differentiate culpable from non-culpable behaviours

There are 2 types of unsatisfactory employee behaviour: culpable and non-culpable.

Culpable Behaviour

Culpable behaviour occurs when:

  • Actions are deliberate and/or intentional and under the employee’s control;
  • The employee knows what is expected, and is capable of performing duties but refuses to do so.
Non‑culpable Behaviour

Non-culpable behaviour occurs due to factors outside the employee’s control, such as lack of skill, ability or training.

  • This type of behaviour warrants a non‑disciplinary approach and the employee must be assisted to achieve satisfactory performance.
  • Management should offer training, support, coaching or counselling to correct the problem.
  • When an employee suffers from a physical or a mental disability, management has a duty to accommodate (see the section on “Performance issues and accommodation” for more details).

Culpable behaviour is considered misconduct and is addressed through the disciplinary process rather than through a performance improvement plan that would be incorporated into an employee’s performance agreement.

Transcript

After conducting a performance management meeting with your employee, has the performance issue improved? If you’ve spoken to the employee about specific behaviours and offered the option of accommodation on several occasions, and the individual does not wish to pursue the matter or indicates that he or she does not require accommodation, document the steps you took to show that you did everything you could to help the employee and that you fulfilled your obligations regarding the duty to accommodate. Be sure to advise the employee of available services such as the Employee Assistance Program.
